Mapping Study: Youth and Civil Society in Urban Cambodia
Abstract

The mapping study on youth and civil society in urban Cambodia has been conducted to understand the current space of Cambodian youths. The study aims to understand the aspirations, opportunities, challenges, concerns, and the needs of youth actors, and potential actors. The purpose is therefore to provide the EUD with a more comprehensive understanding of the diverse landscape of civil society actors in Cambodia committed to empowering urban youth, to foster their participation and agency, their engagement in issues of public interest and in promoting transformative change in the country.

Reinvigorating Cambodian Agriculture: Transforming from Extensive to Intensive Agriculture
Abstract

In this paper we analysis to identify the factor constraining on Cambodian agriculture in transforming from extensive to intensive agriculture. The objective of this study was to examine the general situation of Cambodian agriculture by comparing with neighbouring countries in Southeast Asia from a period of 22 years (1996–2018) through cultivating areas, technical using, technologies using, fertilizer using, agricultural infrastructure system, agricultural production cost, agricultural output, agricultural market and climate change. The results show that the Cambodian agriculture sector is still at a level where there is a significant need to improve the capacity of farmers, the new technologies use and the prevention of climate change. However, the production cost is still high cost and agricultural output has been at low prices. It also causes farmers to lose confidence in farming and they will stop working in the sector. Moreover, we also have other policies to improve agriculture sector in Cambodia.

Monetary Policy and Household Income Distribution: An Empirical Analysis from Cambodia
Abstract

This paper investigates to estimate the distributional effects of monetary policy shocks on macroeconomic aggregates and aggregate consumption. An earning heterogeneity channel, a Fisher channel and an interest rate exposure channel were applied as transmis- sion channels affect aggregate spending when households have different average propen- sities of consume. Through the Structural VAR model, I find that monetary policy shock pursuant to the exchange rate has positive consequences on inflation, real output and the unemployment rate. Simultaneously, sufficient statistics from Cambodian cross-sectional data in the time period 2014–2020 suggests that all three channels are likely to amplify the effects of monetary policy. Furthermore, I discover that the increase in inequality of household consumption and liabilities over the past 7 years, while decreasing household income and assets inequality over the same period.

Government Ownership of Banks: Diversifying of Potential Products and Factors to Subsidize Agriculture
Abstract

It has recently been shown that government ownership of banks has a significant role in addressing market failures, improving social welfare and economic development. This study explores to identify the potential product and factor in agriculture that the public bank should subsidize. In this paper, the author investigates statistical properties of the two-step generalized method of moments (GMM) estimator to analyses the direct and indirect consume of inputs in agricultural production at national-level data for 32 crop products and 14 livestock products from Cambodia during 1989–2018.

Many specifications have statistically significant and negative competent production growth. These results suggest that the proposed subsidy should clearly define the types of speciality products by the local producer and the potential market for both the local and international market. This paper investigates some policies options for government ownership of banks to improve agriculture. However, for the long-run sustainable development in the sector, it should adapt to new climate change and emergency events. Future directions should consider studying micro-data with the specific type of each product and region.

Food Science in Cambodoia
Abstract

In terms of long-term growth prospects, it cannot depend solely on foreign direct investment, the development of domestic firms can contribute to self-growth. The development of domestic firms faces many problems which are financial, technical, technological, low productivity, unable to fix costs. The development of the food industry to be a leading sector in the future as well as to take advantage of agriculture is something that decision-makers have to make up. This paper examines the positive impact of the agricultural sector on the development of food science and technology, the role of R&D in the promotion and development of the food sector, and possible policy responses.

Activity Report: Eco Study Trip 2019
Abstract

This report presents the achievement of the Eco Study 2019, where the trip organized by the student from the French Department of Economics and Management of the Royal University of Law and Economics based in Phnom Penh, Cambodia. Eco Study is a social, environmental, and humanitarian journey to another place in Cambodia ever since the French cooperation founded in 2001, and it continues to be annually up to 18 years.
